## What Is The Exchange?

The Exchange connects employers, students, recent graduates, program completers, and early-career talent through internships, short-term roles, and project-based engagements.

This is not a job board, staffing service, or résumé database. It is a coordinated pathway for meaningful, time-bound work experiences that benefit both employers and emerging talent.

---

## Why The Exchange?

Early-career talent in the region is eager, trained, and ready to contribute, but often lacks access to the applied experience employers look for.

At the same time, many businesses have real projects, operational needs, and emerging skills challenges that can become meaningful learning opportunities with the right structure and support.

The Exchange is designed to make that connection easier, more equitable, and aligned with regional workforce needs.

## For Applicants

Applicants can express interest in hands-on opportunities with regional employers.

The Exchange is for early-career talent, students, recent graduates, program completers, and career changers who are ready to apply their skills in a professional setting.

Candidates do not apply directly to employers through The Exchange. The Exchange team reviews candidate interest, skills, readiness, availability, and alignment with available opportunities.

## How It Works

1. Employers Submit Opportunities
Businesses share a project, internship, or short-term role through The Exchange intake form.

2. Candidates Express Interest
Applicants submit information about their skills, training background, interests, availability, and supporting materials.

3. The Exchange Reviews for Fit
The Exchange team reviews both employer opportunities and candidate profiles to determine readiness and alignment.

4. Matching Begins
Aligned candidates are surfaced to employers from a curated, pre-vetted pool.

5. Work-Based Learning Begins
Once a match is confirmed, the employer and candidate begin the internship, project, or short-term role.

6. Outcomes Are Documented
The Exchange collects feedback from both sides to understand what worked, what can improve, and how the model should evolve.
